PeoPo Visits
Our First-Graders

公視 PeoPo 公民新聞採訪新生報到日
April 2026 · By Citizen Reporter Chou Hou-hsien · 周厚賢

A citizen journalist arrived at the school gate at seven in the morning to cover Huatan Elementary's annual welcome for incoming first-graders. He left calling what he had seen "real education." 公民記者周厚賢在新生報到日清晨七點到校，原本只是要做一則新聞報導。他離開時，把自己看到的事情稱為「最真的教育」。

What PeoPo Saw 公民記者看見的那一幕

The reporter showed up early. The school's First Registration Day was just beginning — an annual ritual where incoming first-graders, most of them six years old, walk through the campus for the first time with their parents. The day usually carries some quiet magic for families.

What the reporter expected to find: the standard handover scene — a registration desk, name tags, an opening speech. What he found instead: the principal herself, at 7:00 a.m., carrying chairs and setting up the welcome stations alongside the teachers.

記者很早就到了。當天是花壇國小新生報到日——一個每年舉辦的儀式，迎接 115 學年度即將進入小學的一年級新生，多數是六歲的孩子，第一次和父母一起走進校園。對家庭來說，這一天總是帶著淡淡的紀念感。

記者原本以為會看到的，是一般學校的標準畫面：報到櫃台、名牌、開場致詞。他實際看到的，卻是清晨七點，校長詹雪梅本人和老師們一起搬椅子、佈置闖關活動的攤位。

I arrived at seven, and saw Principal Chan herself moving things around. I think this is the truest education there is. 那天七點到校，看到詹雪梅校長親自搬東搬西⋯我想這就是最真的教育。
—— 周厚賢，PeoPo 公民記者

What the Day Was Really About 這一天真正想傳遞的

Huatan plans every year's First Registration Day around a different theme. This year's theme came down to a single sentence: show families what their children's six years here will actually look like.

So instead of a static handover, the school turned the morning into a sample of student life. The taiko drum club performed. The orchestra played. The basketball and soccer teams ran demonstrations. Hot-dance, Hokkien-language and Hakka-language classes ran short introduction sessions in real classrooms. By the time families left, they had not just registered — they had seen what kind of place they were entrusting their child to.

花壇國小每年新生報到日都會用不同的主題呈現學校特色。今年的主題濃縮成一句話：讓家長親眼看見，孩子未來六年在這裡的學習，會是什麼樣子。

於是，學校把整個上午從一場「靜態報到」變成一場學生生活的縮影。太鼓社表演、管弦樂團演奏、籃球隊與足球隊現場示範、熱舞社開場、台語與客語老師也走進真實的教室上一段示範課。等家長離開時，他們不只是報完到——他們看見了自己即將把孩子託付給的，是怎樣一個地方。

Why It Mattered 這篇報導的意義

It would be easy to read the PeoPo reporter's words as flattery. They were not. They were a citizen journalist's quiet observation: that an institution shows its real values not in its press release, but in who is carrying the chairs at 7 a.m.

For Huatan, the report became a small mirror. It reminded the school that the parts of a workday too small to plan for — the early arrival, the chair, the smile to a six-year-old — are exactly what visitors notice first. We are grateful PeoPo took the time to look.

很容易把 PeoPo 記者的話當成客套。但那不是。那是一位公民記者安靜的觀察——一個機構真正的樣子，不藏在新聞稿裡，而藏在「誰在清晨七點搬那張椅子」。

這篇報導，對花壇國小來說，是一面小小的鏡子。它提醒我們：那些小到不會被規劃進行事曆的細節——比預定時間早到的那段時間、搬好的那張椅子、對六歲孩子的那個微笑——往往才是外人最先注意到的東西。我們感謝 PeoPo 願意停下腳步看一眼。
